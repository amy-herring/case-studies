Case for Class: Infant Mortality

Infant death is defined as the death of a live-born infant during the first year of life.

The infant mortality rate is the number of infant deaths per 1000 live births. The infant mortality rate, which is relatively easily measured even in resource-poor settings, is often used as a marker of the overall health of a society because of the overlap in causes of infant mortality and factors that are likely to influence the health of an overall population, such as general living conditions, poverty and socioeconomic status, quality of medical care, and rates of illness. Infant mortality rates can be compared across different areas or across time to evaluate the impact of public health program or to determine where new investments in health are needed.

For illustration, we’ll simulate data for North Carolina and plot it on the map.

R code to link map and simulated data

library(dplyr)
##

library(ggplot2)
nc_map <- tbl_df(map_data("county", region = "north carolina"))
# Simulate data to plot by county
data_frame(
  subregion = unique(nc_map$subregion),
  simdata = sample(50:500, length(unique(nc_map$subregion)))
) -> simdata_df
# Join the values to the map
nc_map <- left_join(nc_map, simdata_df)
## Joining, by = "subregion"

Plotting code

library(ggthemes); library(mapproj)
ggplot() +
  geom_polygon(data=nc_map, color="black",
               aes(x=long, y=lat, group=subregion, fill=simdata)) +
  viridis::scale_fill_viridis(name="Simulated Data ", direction=-1) +
  coord_map("polyconic") +
  ggthemes::theme_map() +
  theme(legend.position="bottom")
